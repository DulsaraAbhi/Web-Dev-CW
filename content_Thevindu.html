Quality Education

Education is the process of learning and teaching. It involves gaining knowledge, values and skills through experience, standard learning, observation and etc.

Importance of Education Quality Education SDG explained Targets of quality education SDG

Importance of Education

Education is the foundation for a better world as it is through education that humans develop the abilities to think different, to respect human diversity and to work as a community. Educated people are considered socially and economically strong since they get access to better communities and better opportunities in workforce.

Overall, education equips people with the tools they need to thrive academically, socially, and personally, laying the foundation for a brighter future.

Quality Education SDG explained

  1. The goal focuses on ensuring that all girls and boys have access to free, equitable, and quality education. This means eliminating differences in access to education
  2. It promotes education that respects and celebrates cultural diversity and fosters a sense of belonging for all students.
  3. Also it highlights the importance of training educators.
  4. It promotes the importance of lifelong learning opportunities for people of all ages.
  5. It aims to equip learners with the knowledge, attitudes, and skills necessary to contribute positively to society, respect diversity, and address global challenges collaboratively.

Targets of quality education SDG

  1. Free primary and secondary education.
  2. Equal access to quality pre-primary education.
  3. Equal access to affordable technical, vocational and higher education.
  4. Increase the number of people with relavant skills for financial success.
  5. Eliminate all discrimination education.
  6. Universal literacy and numeracy.
  7. Education for sustainable development and global citizenship.
  8. Build and upgrade inclusive and safe schools.
  9. Expand higher education scholarships for developing countries.
  10. Increase the supply of qualified teachers in developing countries.

Source: The Global Goals
